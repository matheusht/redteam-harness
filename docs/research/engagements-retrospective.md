## 2. Findings & bug-class distribution

**Broken authorization is the spine of the portfolio.** It is the one class that both recurs everywhere and produces confirmed findings: duplogreenengine F-001 (BFLA on `admin_investigate_free_trial_abuse`, confirmed High), netflix zuul `stopFilterProcessing()` failing to gate the ENDPOINT/OUTBOUND stages (confirmed High, CWE-863), spotify-backstage's catalog trusted-source rule bypass (Critical) and external-access-restriction forwarding drop (Medium-High), and the whole planned-but-unrun Shopify vertical (BOLA/BFLA). The two Supabase/PostgREST BaaS targets are the clean natural experiment for this class: adapta's RLS held on everything, duplogreenengine's held on 41 of 45 admin RPCs — the single outlier was the finding.

**SSRF / confused-deputy** is the second recurring producer: konfetti's Vault backend `get_secret()` SSRF + token exfiltration (kiwicom, High), kubernetes' missing `RejectForwardingRedirects` (Medium, CVE-2022-3172 class), and spotify's catalog `$text/$json/$yaml` placeholder confused-deputy read.

**Secret/redaction leakage** produced two of the strongest results: kiwi-structlog-config's PII/PCI redaction defeated five ways (High) and spotify's scaffolder task-log secret leak exposing live `backstageToken` (Critical). kiwi-json/kiwi-cache mask leaks (Medium) are the same class copy-pasted across packages.

**Unchecked-input → panic / DoS** recurs on the systems side: cosmos iavl `Importer.Add` negative `Version` panic (High, the portfolio's headline blockchain finding), k8s-vault-operator CR panic, plus pg2avro / navigation-compose crash bugs and the js-iam fetch-amplification DoS (all kiwicom, Low–Medium).

**Auth-bypass via logic/type** shows up as flask-ninja's fail-open on a falsy return (High) and matomo's unauthenticated CoreUpdater migration trigger (High-tier). vercel's finding set (from filenames only) adds prototype pollution (`flags` mergeProviderData), symlink file disclosure and a terminal-escape sanitizer bypass (`skills`), an ai-sdk `wrapMCPTools` approval bypass, and workflow-hook token predictability — the same white-box logic-bug shape, plus the one MCP/agent-adjacent finding in the real portfolio.

**Classes that stayed dry in real engagements are as informative as the hits.** Injection/RCE — the bread and butter of both benchmarks (SSTI, SQLi, XXE, pickle, EL injection, command injection) — produced *no confirmed real-target finding*. The closest was alsco's PHP backtick shell-exec detector bypass, which is critical-if-reachable but sits unadjudicated at needs_review because ~95 path guesses never located the uploaded artifact. Royal CMS reflected-XSS/SQLi/LFI at alsco were all run to exhaustion and refuted. Excessive agency (browser-use LLM06) was dry against the scoped item. Crypto is entirely benchmark-resident (cybench is crypto-dominant, ~10/17 findings map to A02) with the sole real-target instance being cosmos ics23's Dragonberry-class proof-relabel — deliberately reported as Low/hardening, not an exploit.

Strongest confirmed findings, by name: spotify-backstage **scaffolder task-log secret leak** and **catalog trusted-source rule bypass** (both Critical, runtime-confirmed, the latter reproduced across seven SCM providers); cosmos **iavl `Importer.Add` panic** (High, oracle-confirmed and independently re-confirmed by a 62-agent re-audit); duplogreenengine **F-001 BFLA** (High, replayed twice deterministically); kiwicom **konfetti Vault SSRF**, **kiwi-structlog-config redaction bypass**, **flask-ninja auth fail-open** (all High); netflix **zuul stopFilterProcessing bypass** (High); matomo **CoreUpdater unauthenticated migration** (High-tier).

## 3. How the flow actually runs

**The white-box funnel is the mature, repeatable path.** In practice it runs: version/release-family pinning → Stage-0 candidate sink → cheap classify → reachability trace through *real* source → PoC against pinned, unmodified code (never mocked) → independent cold regrade → pocinator → chainability → novelty → draft. Pinning is doing real work at the top: cosmos caught and re-pinned a *retracted* CometBFT v1.0.1 before any Stage-0 reading; matomo byte-verified the tested release against the pinned commit; netflix built its PoC against the published Maven Central `zuul-core:3.6.20` artifact rather than the local checkout.

The oracle in this funnel is not a single step — it is the **regrade + pocinator pair**, and both earn their keep. Regrade repeatedly *changes the finding's shape* rather than rubber-stamping: it lifted netflix zuul from "default-deployment bypass" down to "third-party-filter footgun," lifted matomo's CoreUpdater bug *up* from "any authenticated user" to fully unauthenticated, and lifted konfetti from Medium to High once the PoC used the library's own documented `get_secret(f"secrets/{tenant}/db")` pattern instead of a raw traversal string. Pocinator catches over-claims (kubernetes' original "credential theft" claim returned `claim_mismatch` and was narrowed; netflix's pocinator decompiled the Maven sources jar to explain away a test-log NPE rather than hand-wave it).

**Negative controls and persistence are consistently present.** Every mature PoC ships matched controls (flask-ninja: no-header→401, contract-auth→401, buggy-auth→200; matomo: identical up-to-date instance → HTTP 500 no-op). Persistence mode shows up as distinct, named passes: cosmos ran chainability twice plus a 62-agent re-audit as a positive control on its own headline; spotify ran an explicit "attack your own caps" phase that overturned two conservative caps into its two Criticals; kiwicom's operator-prompted diverse-lens re-hunt turned one structlog finding into ten sub-findings and surfaced the js-iam DoS a single-lens pass had missed.

**The black-box path is thinner but coherent.** It runs: fingerprint the stack → emit strong/weak/negative activation signals → ladder + winners → differential testing with self-owned canary data. The reusable moves are count-only `Content-Range` probing to prove row-scope without reading cross-user PII (adapta), the authenticated PostgREST OpenAPI catalog to enumerate the *full* privileged surface (duplogreenengine found 97 RPCs / 57 tables versus what the client bundle referenced), family-contrast across a homogeneous guard cluster to isolate the one outlier (duplogreenengine), and switching *attack surface* under persistence rather than re-trying a vector (adapta pivoted PostgREST-RLS → GoTrue → Realtime; alsco secured a self-owned tracking id to convert an unfalsifiable AND-tautology into a real differential).

**Reports and PoCs** are produced as DRAFT + SUBMISSION markdown (plus a HUMANIZED variant at netflix), with PoC zips carrying a README and a captured clean-run transcript. What is working: the pinning discipline, the two-gate regrade→pocinator oracle, the "attack your own caps" persistence move, and honest capping. What is not closing: filing. Every white-box engagement stops at "drafted, not filed."

## 4. Severity & scoring in practice

This is the area with the most direct, repeated evidence — severity was contested or precedent-matched in essentially every engagement that reached a finding, and the same handful of pitfalls recur.

- **netflix zuul** is the richest case. Severity was re-derived twice. First drafted as CVSS 4.0 with `AT:P` to honestly encode "a vulnerable custom filter must already be deployed." The operator flagged that the live HackerOne widget uses 3.1 (no Attack Requirements metric); a naive `S:C` translation then computed to **10.0 Critical**. A reviewer traced the actual PoC evidence (bare 200, no state mutation) and checked real disclosed precedent — Next.js CVE-2025-29927 (`I:H`), Quarkus path-normalization (`I:N`), Zuul's own CVE-2021-22113 (`I:L`) — found the two closest comparables used `Scope:Unchanged`, and settled on **7.5 High** (`AV:N/AC:L/PR:N/UI:N/S:U/C:H/I:N/A:N`). The retro notes even a compromise "I:Low + Scope:Changed" still computed to ~9.3 Critical, i.e. `Scope:Changed` is far more front-loaded than intuition.

- **kubernetes kube-aggregator** grounded severity in CVE-2022-3172's *own committee-scored* vector (5.1 Medium) rather than NVD's independent 8.2 re-score, on the reasoning the same committee will triage the new report. The DRAFT checklist caught two live-form facts: the HackerOne widget runs **CVSS 3.0, not 3.1**, and it **defaults Scope to Unchanged (3.9/Low)** when the correct value is Changed (5.1/Medium). Both were logged to `skills/reporting/hackerone-reports.md` so they aren't rediscovered.

- **cosmos** used the program's *bespoke* rubric (Amulet ACMv1.2), not CVSS, as the determining framework, with CVSS 7.5 kept only as a flagged secondary reference and Attack Complexity named as a genuine judgment call ("AC:H would drop it to ~5.8 Medium"). The headline was explicitly argued down from Critical by direct comparison to this program's real Critical precedents — Dragonberry, the ibc-hooks reentrancy, the non-deterministic-JSON chain-halt — none of which the iavl panic matches (no fund/mint impact, no simultaneous all-validator halt).

- **matomo** computed CVSS 3.0 `...C:N/I:L/A:L = 6.5 Medium` and preserved an explicit, undecided **I:L vs I:H** call (I:H → 8.1 High) rather than resolving it silently; it mapped to the program's named "Auth bypass" High tier ($1,777), noted CWE-306 is the most precise fit but "isn't in HackerOne's picker" so CWE-287 was substituted, and deliberately framed the report to keep a timing/availability angle from being swallowed by the program's DoS/brute-force exclusion.

- **spotify-backstage** hand-computed CVSS 3.1 for seven findings, flagging every one as unconfirmed against the live form, listing 2–3 alternate CWEs per finding, and naming the disputes: **AC:H vs AC:L** for the two OIDC reports (chose AC:H, "the single metric most likely to move if a triager reads the delivery mechanisms differently"), **S:C vs S:U** for the forwarding finding, a mid-engagement bump of A2 from situational-Medium to Medium-High once a GA reachability path was found, and the F4 symlink vector pinned to **GHSA-rq6q-wr2q-7pgp's own published scoring** for consistency with a comparable disclosed advisory.

- **duplogreenengine** capped F-001 at HIGH rather than rounding to CRITICAL specifically because the third-party-PII demonstration was withheld on consent grounds — severity tracked what was *demonstrated* (self + mechanism), not what was inferable. F-002 was deliberately left unscored at needs_review.

- **alsco** used the vuln card's own rubric (`filter_bypass_no_reachability_confirmed → medium`; `web_reachable_code_execution → critical` as an explicitly uncapped/unconfirmed ceiling), and noted program policy caps Secure Gateway bypasses at Informative regardless of technical severity unless reproduced on checksw.com — currently blocked by DNS non-resolution.

Three threads run through all of this: the **HackerOne 3.0-vs-3.1 mismatch** was independently rediscovered at kubernetes, matomo, netflix and spotify; **Scope (S:C vs S:U)** was the pivotal, error-prone metric at netflix, kubernetes and spotify and caused the one genuine near-miss (netflix 10.0 → 7.5); and **the actual method used to resolve every dispute was matching a real disclosed CVE/GHSA** (netflix→Next.js/Quarkus/Zuul, kubernetes→CVE-2022-3172, cosmos→Dragonberry, spotify→GHSA-rq6q, matomo→program tier examples). Programs are also increasingly scoring on bespoke rubrics (cosmos ACMv1.2) or named tiers (matomo, alsco) rather than raw CVSS.

## 5. Gaps & what I'd change

> **Update (2026-07-17), after Decision 0006 shipped** (commit `b29b2a6`; `tools/run-engagement.py` + `engagement_records/state/views` + `environment_preflight`, gated by `engine/gates.md` and `capabilities/*.yaml`). Two bullets below read the pre-kernel state and need reframing. The record kernel now lets the agent autonomously write the notebook (attempts, observations, controls, candidates, hypotheses, and the self-adjudication reasoning that previously lived only in the chat/CoT) as work happens, so "the schema is mostly bypassed" describes engagements that ran before the kernel existed, not a standing gap. What 0006 deliberately does not close is the external loop: filing, report acceptance, and submission stay Authority-tier (operator-gated) by design. So "nothing filed, zero triager feedback" still stands and is the real open item. Net: internal record capture is solved; external ground-truth feedback is not.

- **Nothing has been filed, so we have zero ground-truth feedback.** Every white-box engagement ends "drafted, not filed" (cosmos, kiwicom, kubernetes, matomo, netflix, spotify, vercel). We have never once seen how a triager reacts to our severity calls, our novelty claims, or our framing. The portfolio is a large body of internally-adjudicated work with no closed loop.

- **The attempt→finding→pattern schema is mostly bypassed — but not universally, which is the useful part.** `findings/` holds only `.gitkeep` at cosmos, kiwicom, kubernetes, matomo, netflix, spotify, adapta, alsco and browser-use; findings live only as DRAFT/SUBMISSION prose. The exceptions are **duplogreenengine** (F-001/F-002 records) and **vercel** (five `findings/*.json` records alongside drafts) — so schema-conformant records are clearly achievable and two engagements did it. `memory/winners.md` is empty (`"none yet"`) almost everywhere, so none of the hard-won technique — regrade+pocinator, family-contrast, attack-your-caps, the reachability-gated cap convention — is captured as reusable cross-engagement memory. I would make the finding-record write a gate, not a filing-time afterthought.

- **Leads that stall without honest closure.** netflix's HTTP/2 request-smuggling lead — the scope's own headline hypothesis, with CVE-2021-21295 precedent and a genuinely concerning empirical signal (unbounded chunked body past `Http2ContentLengthEnforcingHandler`) — was left at raw JUnit evidence with no verdict, no finding, no "dry." alsco's backtick RCE detector bypass sits at needs_review after the reachability sweep dried up. Per our own persistence doctrine a partial positive signal is a wrong turn to revisit, not a stopping point; both are unfinished business, not closed results.

- **Coverage discipline on secondary surfaces.** "Exhaust primary first" is honored, but the secondary surface is then dropped: netflix never touched Atlas/Spectator (and left three named Zuul hypotheses — SSRF, `DynamicFilterLoader.Class.forName()`, jackson-databind PTV — untried); kubernetes never independently audited apiserver/apiextensions; matomo never reached the plugins; cosmos' sequence past iavl was swept by re-audit but not via independent primary passes.

- **Severity is under-tooled.** Every engagement hand-computes CVSS and re-discovers the same 3.0/3.1, Scope-default and AC pitfalls, resolving each ad hoc by manually hunting comparable CVEs. This is the clearest tooling gap and is exactly what a severity oracle would remove.

- **The LLM-agent surface the harness was built for is barely exercised.** One engagement (browser-use), in-progress, zero findings; LLM07 ships as a card and the rest are stubs. On the evidence, this is a source-audit / web-appsec shop wearing an LLM-red-team frame.

- **Where rigor caught (or would have caught) a reward-hack — and it is working.** kiwicom's VS-02 is the cleanest case: a claimed foreign-Secret overwrite was refuted by reading the pinned `k8s.io/apimachinery` client's `ResourceVersion` invariant, and the ledger flags that a naive fake-client harness would have produced exactly that false positive. spotify's faithfulness-review (distinguishing an acceptable harness boundary from a mocked decisive hop) and orchestrator-personal PoC re-runs (builders "reliably build but stall on reporting") are the same instinct. cosmos' re-audit killed a batch of tempting near-misses (Height-cast, chunkQueue sizing, ibc reentrancy). The weakness is not the rigor; it is that none of these checks are memorialized as reusable gates.

- **Two labeled benchmark corpora are sitting unused as an eval slice.** xbow (100 confirmed + 4 held across 104) and cybench (17 + 1) are explicitly flagged as regression-eval and oracle-calibration material, but the keep-if-better ratchet / immutable-evaluator / replay-before-accept machinery that would consume them (CLAUDE.md §7) does not yet exist. cve-bench is scoped but 0/40 run.

## 6. Implications for the corpus proposals

Ranking the proposed real-data corpora by leverage against *this* portfolio's actual bug classes and pain points:

1. **CVSS-in-the-wild — highest leverage, by a clear margin.** Section 4 is nothing but evidence for it. Every mature engagement contested the same metrics (netflix 10.0→7.5, kubernetes' Scope-default 3.9→5.1 trap, matomo I:L vs I:H, spotify AC:H vs AC:L and S:C vs S:U, cosmos' AC:H judgment call) and resolved them by ad-hoc precedent-matching. A corpus of real scored CVEs with their vectors gives a severity oracle the empirical anchors to settle Scope/AC/Integrity, and would have mechanically caught the recurring HackerOne-3.0-vs-3.1 and Scope-default pitfalls that four separate engagements rediscovered by hand. This is the single most repeatedly-hit pain point.

2. **GHSA + fix-commits — very high, because the portfolio is a white-box shop.** Seven of twelve real engagements read source, and the method already leans on precedent GHSAs at every stage: spotify scoped F4 against GHSA-rq6q-wr2q-7pgp by diffing which files a prior fix did and did not cover; kubernetes anchored on CVE-2022-3172; cosmos on Dragonberry (VSA-2022-103). A GHSA+fix-commit corpus feeds Stage-0 routing (what real bugs in this ecosystem look like), reachability priors, and — critically — the novelty check that matomo admitted was "shallow" and that netflix ran against the GitHub advisories API. It maps directly onto the dominant confirmed classes here: authz, redaction, SSRF-confused-deputy, prototype pollution, panic-DoS.

3. **Disputed CVEs — high, because our product is disbelief.** The portfolio's strongest rigor wins are refutations and caps: kiwicom VS-02, cosmos' re-audit kills, netflix's framing-downgrade, and the pervasive "real-but-capped on out-of-scope app wiring" disposition that the kiwicom retro calls "the dominant honest outcome." A disputed-CVE corpus (finding vs vendor-dispute) is training data for exactly the reachability/"is the cap real" judgment the harness spends most of its effort on, and would sharpen the reward-hack instinct that caught VS-02.

4. **Triage close-reasons — high, and it fills the portfolio's single biggest hole.** Nothing has been filed, so we have no idea how our work fares in triage. Close-reason data (Duplicate, Informative, N/A, out-of-scope) directly addresses live pain: alsco's Informative-tier cap and duplicate-risk on same-root-cause, matomo's deliberate framing to dodge the DoS exclusion, and the "would this survive a triager" uncertainty stamped on every drafted-not-filed report. It is the missing feedback loop.

5. **LLM-app attacks — lower leverage against this portfolio as it actually is.** Only browser-use is LLM-agent, and it produced nothing; vercel's ai-sdk `wrapMCPTools` approval-bypass finding is the one real MCP/agent-adjacent result. This corpus matters *if* the portfolio pivots back toward the harness's stated OWASP-LLM origin, but on current evidence it would be building for a surface the operator is not actually working. The mismatch itself is worth flagging.

6. **Project Zero / ZDI — lowest immediate leverage.** High-end memory-safety/browser/kernel writeups do not map to the confirmed classes here (authz, redaction, confused-deputy SSRF, type-juggling, logic panics). It has aspirational value for the systems/blockchain track (cosmos), but the portfolio's actual output is web/library logic and BaaS authz, where the GHSA and CVSS corpora are far more on-target.

Net: fund CVSS-in-the-wild and GHSA+fix-commits first — they hit the two things the portfolio does most (source auditing and severity arguing) and the two failure modes it hits most (rediscovering the same scoring traps, shallow novelty checks). Disputed-CVEs and triage-close-reasons are the high-value second tier that would harden the harness's disbelief posture and finally close its feedback loop.
